Sci-Fi

1. Code Geass

Code Geass is an anime series about exiled Britannian prince Lelouch vi Britannia, who gains a mysterious power called Geass and uses it to lead a rebellion against the oppressive Holy Britannian Empire. He becomes the masked leader Zero and plots to create a world where his disabled sister, Nunnally, can live happily, while also pursuing his vow to destroy Britannia. The plot explores themes of power, rebellion, and the moral consequences of Lelouch's strategic and often brutal actions.

2. Tokyo Revengers

Tokyo Revengers is an anime about a struggling man named Takemichi Hanagaki who, after a shocking event, finds himself traveling back in time to his middle school days. He decides to use this opportunity to prevent the death of his ex-girlfriend, Hinata Tachibana, who is killed by the violent Tokyo Manji Gang. To do this, he must infiltrate the gang and work his way up to change its violent future, navigating gang wars and betrayals along the way.

3. Dr. Stone

The Dr. Stone anime is about a 17-year-old genius named Senku who wakes up 3,700 years after a mysterious light petrifies all of humanity. He uses his knowledge of science to rebuild civilization, eventually reviving his friend Taiju. The story progresses as Senku and his allies, the "Kingdom of Science," work to revive others and confront the opposing force of "Tsukasa Empire," which wants to eliminate all adults and create a new world with only the young.

4. The Promised Neverland

The Promised Neverland is a mystery and horror anime about orphans who discover their idyllic orphanage is actually a farm raising them as food for demons. The children, led by Emma, Norman, and Ray, must use their intelligence to plan an escape from their loving caretaker, "Mom," and their fate. The first season focuses on this tense, strategic battle of wits against their "mother".
