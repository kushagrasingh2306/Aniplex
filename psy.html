1. Death Note

Death Note is a 2006 Japanese anime series about a brilliant high school student, Light Yagami, who finds a supernatural notebook that kills anyone whose name is written in it. He uses it to kill criminals in an attempt to create a crime-free world, but a genius detective known only as "L" works to stop him, setting the stage for a gripping psychological battle of wits. The series explores complex themes of justice, vigilantism, and morality.

2. Monster

The plot of the anime Monster follows a brilliant neurosurgeon, Dr. Kenzo Tenma, who saves a young boy named Johan Liebert's life over the mayor's, an act of conscience that derails his career. Nine years later, Tenma discovers that the boy he saved has grown up to become a charismatic and manipulative serial killer. Realizing the danger he unleashed on the world, Tenma embarks on a cross-country hunt to track down and stop Johan, becoming a suspect in the serial murders along the way. The story is a psychological thriller that explores themes of good versus evil, and how even well-intentioned actions can have disastrous consequences.
